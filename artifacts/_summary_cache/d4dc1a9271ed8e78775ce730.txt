This Usenet post discusses historical Armenian perspectives on the genocide and ethnic cleansing in Armenia, comparing it to the Holocaust. It references various memoirs that describe systematic extermination of Turkish and Muslim populations by Armenian authorities and military forces. The author cites specific quotes from Armenian sources, including "Men Are Like That" by an Armenian army officer, which describes the organized genocide. The post also provides historical context about Armenia's population distribution in 1886, noting Armenians constituted a minority even in areas where they were initially dominant. It concludes with a quote from Ohanus Appressian describing the aftermath of these events as silent and desolate villages dotted along the Armenian borderlands.
